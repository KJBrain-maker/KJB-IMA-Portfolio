Self-Portrait 2.0

Course — Creative Coding Completed — July 12, 2026 Tags — Code

Here is my updated self-portrait, built in p5.js.

For this version of the portrait, I wanted to add more shapes and texture to the hair. I really loved Daniel's version when I saw his in class and he talked about how difficult the hair was to do, so I thought it would be fun to add some more hair since I looked pretty bald previously. And then I wanted to do something with the eyes, since it had taken so long for me to get all of the circles for the eyes accurate I figured I could take the highlight and move it around and it could look like she's watching you. So, that's what I used my mouseX & mouseY for, and it took me forever.

Coding is not coming naturally, and it is a lot of trial and error for me since I do not love math. But making the drawing of the axis really did help. Reviewing and figuring out the order of everything and what I needed to amend was difficult. I had an idea to make her blink randomly, but that was much harder than I anticipated, and once I got it to work, it looked like one eyeball was glitching, so I decided to change the background and make it go random…like a party!

I definitely attempted to Google "How do I add mouseX…" but that was NOT helpful. So, I went back to my notes and I figured it out. The code is a mess and I got a little mixed up on what to delete from my attempt at blinking so some of that is just there…I guess I could delete it but I guess in showing process…there it all is. Messy. Frustrating but in the end…kinda satisfying.

lol. Just went back to the lesson worksheet…WELL AFTER I finished, and so many answers were right there when my notes failed me. I am still organizing my life in Notion so hopefully it will be easier this week.

Lastly, I find it hard to find things in the library. Mostly because I have to think in the simplest of terms. What am I trying to make, and start with a shape and then another shape, instead of "make a brow with a fierce arch over the left eye." No, no, no. Many more steps than that. It is like knowing how to ask where the bathroom is in Spanish but really wanting to ask someone on a date.
