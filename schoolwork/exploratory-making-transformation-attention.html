Exploration 2: Transformation Through Attention

Course — Exploratory Making Status — Completed

In reading "Deep Listening: A Composer's Sound Practice," I immediately pause and end up holding my breath when I think about listening. In November 2024, I got a virus — maybe it was Covid, maybe it was something else. But it left me with tinnitus, so anytime I really stop and slow down, I'm filled with this sort of anxiety about what I'll hear when I really listen to my body, my own breath, and my own mind. But it is exactly these exercises that help calm my vagus nerve and lessen the effects of the tinnitus.

As an actor and a dancer, we always do a lot of these relaxation methods. So I started with the listening exercise. I sat in a chair and fixed my posture, and just felt how every bone, every ligament, every piece of myself fit together — the parts that are stacked on top of each other, the parts that are connected with tissue and cartilage. I felt for the uncomfortable places. I listened to the quiet ringing that always accompanies my silence. I listened to the fan, and the sirens, and the voices, and the distractions. And then I pulled my focus to my heart, the blood pumping through my veins, reminding me I'm alive.

And then I did arm swinging — forward and back, with the prescribed bend in the knees for circulation. I try to do this in the mornings when I wake up. I always feel so much better when I stretch my body and do lymphatic drainage first thing. I do it…but not consistently. Maybe this will help me remember.

Lastly, I really liked the idea of massaging my organs, so I did the "Wrap and Slap." I tried some of these while I was taking my dog out for a walk. I had also tried the extremely slow walk with my dog and it did not go well — we had very different goals.

Sometimes I think that because of this tinnitus, I overstimulate and distract myself so that I don't have to hear it, but it's the moments that I can relax, listen, psychologically, and voluntarily that everything starts to fade away and I can feel more myself again. Apparently it's mostly "stress related," say all of my doctors, so perhaps slowing down and really listening to myself is the step I need to take consistently.

— KJB
