Interface Labs · Week 2

Fantasy Device: Eyes Are the Window to the Heart

Course — Interface Labs Status — In progress Tags — Design

Group Project 1 — with Emily Lu and Sophia Tiutan.

Project Prompt

World: communication never happens in person. Social/device purpose: time spent with other people is currency. Interaction points/gesture/body: blinking, eyes. You: a beginner — in love.

Process

Step 1: gathering our supplies! We gravitated toward eyeglasses because of the blinking interaction point in our prompt. Step 2: planning out our device details and circuit design. Step 3: assembling the "love booth."

What

We're building a love booth activated by love glasses. This device is set in a re-imagined post-COVID world where people have lost the ability to speak due to the lack of in-person social activities, causing humans to devolve and communicate only through non-verbal means, particularly blinking in morse code.

The love booth involves two participants and is formatted like a confessional — separate entrances for each participant, with a partition in between. Participant A enters one side and places the "key to the heart" on the sensor to unlock and begin the experience. Both participants wear glasses fitted with sensors: when a participant blinks and their lashes brush against the glasses, it triggers a light on the opposite side of the partition, on the other participant's side of the booth.

Who

A maximum of two people can interact with this device, although future prototypes can encourage larger social experiences.

When

This is a booth for people just trying to find love — think of it like a blind date for non-verbal communicators. We ourselves are beginners in love, trying to find a romantic connection with anyone willing to engage. The device can be used for as long as both parties are interested in continuing to connect.

Where

In this post-apocalyptic world, these devices are available to the public and have replaced "outdated" hangout venues such as bars and cafes.

Why

This project explores what it means to find a sense of connection in a world where physical contact has ceased to exist. How do we continue to socialize as relational beings when physical constraints exist? Can we find connection without doing so face to face? It also draws from the idea that communication is a two-way street: both participants must be willing to engage for the booth to work, with the lover wearing the glasses and the love recipient holding the key.

How

To interact with this device, you must have "given your heart" to someone through a physical key that unlocks the receiver's booth. Only then can the two of you participate. The quiet, dark rooms of the love booth create a sense of intimacy between the two lovers — no one else can witness the experience, and the participants are in rooms void of distractions, focused only on the connection they're making.

In another iteration, adding visual elements like a video feed would make the experience richer. There's also something quite stale about morse-code-delivered messages, so an auto-translator that gives the messages more personality and emotion could create a deeper connection.
